Getting Started

  • Enable location permissions for real-time friend tracking and map accuracy
  • Turn on “Share my location” to appear on the live festival map
  • Add friends using your unique friend code, or “Bump to connect” in person
  • Star artists to build your personal schedule — My Day follows the current festival day automatically; tap a day to browse another one
  • On first open, Festi picks the festival closest to today’s date — switch anytime in Settings, where each festival shows its dates

Location & Battery

  • Pick an accuracy/battery profile in Account → Accuracy & battery — Low, Balanced, or High
  • When your battery drops below ~20%, Festi automatically switches to Low accuracy to save power (and back once you charge); a notice in Account shows when this happens
  • “Only when I’m at the festival” (on by default) pauses sharing when you’re more than ~1 km from a venue — friends won’t see you at your hotel
  • Friends’ pins show a direction arrow while they’re walking, and a soft circle when their GPS fix is coarse — that circle is the position uncertainty, not a “range”

Common Issues

  • Location not updating: ensure “Always Allow” location access is enabled
  • Friends can’t see me: confirm location sharing is enabled and the friendship is accepted. If “Only when I’m at the festival” is on (it’s the default), you only appear within ~1 km of a venue — turn it off in Account to share anywhere
  • No “friend is at my stage” alerts: the “Alert when a friend joins my stage” toggle must be on, on both phones — the receiver’s toggle decides whether they get the push. Also check system notification permissions for Festi
  • Missing notifications: check system notification permissions for Festi
  • A friend request looks stuck on “pending”: open the Friends tab (or bring the app back to the foreground) — it re-syncs with the server each time
  • Map not loading: restart the app or verify your network connection

Blocking & reporting someone

You can cut off contact with anyone, and flag behaviour to us. You never need permission first, and the other person is not told you reported them.

  • Block a user: Friends tab → tap the friend to open their menu → Block. Blocking removes the friendship and stops them re-adding you: they can no longer see your location, your stats, or your profile. You can unblock someone later from Settings
  • Report a user: from the same menu choose 🚩 Report. You can then pick Report, or Report & block if you also want them cut off. Reporting on its own does not block them — choose “Report & block” for that. Reports go only to the app operator and are reviewed within 24 hours
  • Urgent or serious harm: email dhackenbrook@gmail.com with the friend code involved. If you are in danger at the festival, contact festival security or your local emergency number first — Festi is not an emergency service

Find nearby (Bluetooth)

  • What it does: when GPS puts your friend “somewhere over there” but you still can’t see them, Find Nearby uses a Bluetooth signal between your phones to give you a hotter/colder meter as you walk. It works in a crowd, where GPS usually doesn’t
  • They are told: the friend you’re finding gets a “someone’s finding you” notification. Finding a friend is never silent
  • It needs: Bluetooth on, on both phones, and location sharing on for both of you. It only ever works between accepted friends
  • If it isn’t working: check Bluetooth is on and Festi has Bluetooth permission; make sure you’re both sharing location; and get within roughly 30–50 m — the signal can’t reach across the whole site. The meter shows a rough zone, not a precise distance: bodies and pockets absorb the signal, so it will jitter

Badges

  • How they work: badges are a friendly competition between you and your accepted friends. Each goes to whoever tops it — most time at Mainstage, most stages visited, most ground walked, and so on. Your stats and badges are visible to your accepted friends
  • They need location sharing on: badges are worked out from your shared location. With sharing off, nothing accrues
  • They only count at the festival: time on your sofa doesn’t count — stats accrue only while you’re actually at the venue
  • Nothing moving? stats save about once a minute, so give it a minute. Check sharing is on, and that you’re not in “Only when I’m at the festival” mode while away from the site
  • Turning it off: turn location sharing off and stats stop accruing. Deleting your account removes your stats and badges entirely

Account & Data

You can fully manage your data inside the app:

Account → Privacy & data → Delete account & data

Deleting your account permanently removes your profile, friends, shared location, activity stats, and any blocks or reports you created. A fresh, empty account is created so you can keep using the app.
